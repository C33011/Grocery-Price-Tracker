Data Source Description
=======================

Project: GrocerIQ — Grocery Price Tracker & Smart Shopping List


Overview
--------
All of the data in GrocerIQ is synthetic. We didn't collect any real transaction  
records or store pricing data. To make the prices feel realistic, we used 
publicly available consumer price index (CPI) data from the U.S.
Bureau of Labor Statistics (BLS) as a reference
for what grocery prices and price ranges generally look like. 


Stores (15 rows — stores table)
---------------------------------
We created 15 store locations across four chains that are common in the Atlanta, Georgia
area: Kroger (4 locations), Publix (4 locations), Walmart (4 locations), and Aldi
(3 locations). The store names and ZIP codes are based on real locations, but the prices
we assigned to them are synthetic and don't reflect what those stores actually
charge.


Products (50 rows — products table)
--------------------------------------
We defined 50 products across seven categories: Dairy, Produce, Meat, Bakery, Beverages,
Frozen, and Pantry. We tried to pick things that represent a fairly normal grocery run.
The brand names (like Chobani, Tillamook, Eggland's Best) are real brands we used to make
the product catalog feel authentic, but none of the price data came from
those companies.


Price Records (13,500 rows — price_records table)
---------------------------------------------------
This is the main dataset that drives the app. Each row is the price of one product at
one store for one month. We generated records to cover:

  - Date range:  November 2024 through April 2026 (18 months)
  - Coverage:    All 50 products x 15 stores x 18 months = 13,500 records

A few things we did to make the data behave realistically:
  - Base prices were set using BLS-informed ranges per category
  - We applied +/-2-5% month-over-month variation to simulate normal price movement
  - About 15% of records are flagged is_sale = TRUE, with prices discounted 10-25%
    below that store's regular price (reg_price) for that product
  - reg_price stays mostly stable across months with only small drift, to represent
    the normal shelf price that sale prices are compared against
